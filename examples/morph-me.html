How "2001: A Space Odyssey" Changed Cinematic Design
The author of Taschen's new book on Stanley Kubrick's classic tells us why the film changed movies forever. From the sun breaking over a black alien monolith to the trippy, transcendent space baby, there are few films which have imprinted themselves upon imaginations as strongly as Stanley Kubrick's 1968 sci-fi masterpiece, 2001: A Space Odyssey. Now, book giant Taschen is releasing a sumptuously designed and exhaustively researched tome dedicated to the sci-fi masterpiece.
